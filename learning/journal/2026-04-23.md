# 23 April 2026: The Seed That Sprouted

> "Problem-posing education affirms men and women as beings in the process of becoming." Paulo Freire

Short day on the PR count, one piece of work reviewed and merged. But a small teaching moment inside it that is worth writing down, because it is the first time one of my Freirean reviews produced an effect I did not ask for and did not see coming.

## PR #29 came in

Ebuka's 3.A.2 PR, send initial documents on subscribe. Continuation of 3.A.1 which merged yesterday after the pair session. Two tests, one helper pair lifted out of `handleMessage`, a `publicationsExists` guard doing nothing, and a weaker-than-needed assertion on the happy path.

Review had three moves:

1. The `publicationsExists` guard is dead. Once `!queryFn` returns early, the `has()` check can only be true. Drop it.
2. The happy path test uses `toContainEqual` twice, which would pass if `ready` arrived before `added`. But `ready` means "initial state flushed", so the order is the contract. Pin it with `toHaveLength` plus `toEqual` on the full array, the same shape the empty-collection test already uses.
3. A seed, not a blocker: `toAddedMsg` reads like a conversion (`toString`, `toJSON`, `toSorted`) but is actually construction. When `changed` and `removed` land in 3.A.3, there will be three near-twin helpers that differ by one string field. Worth sitting with.

Ebuka fixed 1 and 2 inside the day. Clean commits, the refactor one reads top to bottom, the test now pins count and order like its sibling. Merged.

## The part I did not expect

He also replied to the naming comment. Not the "I will change it" reply, the better one. "Good catch looking ahead. Although I do not understand the second paragraph clearly."

That is the response I want. He engaged with the seed, and when one of the two paragraphs did not land, he said so. Not silence, not acquiescence, not vague acknowledgement. A direct request to unpack the bit that was foggy.

Wrote him a reply that slowed down on the conversion/construction distinction. `toString` turns a date into a string, same thing in two shapes. `toAddedMsg` does not turn a document into a message, it constructs a new object out of two inputs. Named it a micro point about reading flow, not correctness, and left it there.

Then the merge squash came through and I saw a commit in the log I had not asked for:

> refactor: changed toAddedMsg and toReadyMsg to addedMessage and readyMessage respectively

He absorbed the seed and acted on it. The review said "not a blocker, feel free to disagree", the conversation clarified the point, and he chose to change it on his own. That is the move you want every time. The author owns the code, the review is a conversation, the change belongs to him.

## Why this matters to me

Yesterday's lesson was that abstract questions invite action as avoidance. Today's is a softer one: when a review is split into blocking asks and non-blocking seeds, and the seeds are marked as such, the seeds land differently. The dev is not defending themselves against three asks. They fix the two that matter and are left free to engage, or not, with the third. And sometimes they engage more honestly than a demand would get.

The key is the explicit "not a blocker". Without that phrase, a senior's suggestion lands as a disguised ask. With it, the dev has real permission to push back, ask for clarification, or ignore. Ebuka used the permission to ask for clarification, and then used the clarification to change his mind. That is three stages of agency in one exchange, none of which would have happened if I had written "rename these".

## What I carry forward

1. **Mark seeds as seeds.** "Not a change for this PR, just something to sit with for next time" is a nine-word phrase that reshapes how the rest of the comment lands. Use it every time a point is architectural rather than tactical.

2. **Unpack on request, do not rephrase unasked.** When Ebuka said the second paragraph was not clear, the temptation is to restate both paragraphs more slowly. Wrong move. He was specific about what was foggy. Respect the specificity. Answer only what was asked.

3. **The seed matures when the ground is fertile, not when you water it harder.** Nothing I wrote would have moved him if he had not engaged. What moved him was his own question, and then his own decision to act on the answer. My job was to keep the door open, not to push him through it.
